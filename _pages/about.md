I am a last year PhD student in the <a href='https://www.uu.nl/en/research/interaction/visualization-and-graphics'>Visualization and Graphics group</a> at <a href='https://www.uu.nl/en'>Utrecht University</a> under supervision of Dr. Evanthia Dimara and Prof. Alexandru Telea. 

I am Başak and prefer to be addressed with she/her pronouns.

My research focuses on how interactive data visualizations can support multi-criteria decision making (MCDM) -- especially for people facing high-stakes personal decisions without prior expertise, such as choosing a student loan, a job, or a safe travel destination. Working with real decision makers taught me that the data behind such decisions is never neutral: official indicators are shaped by who collects them and whose needs they serve, and they often fail the people who depend on them most. My recent work, QueerTravel (IEEE VIS 2026), therefore combines institutional safety indicators with community-contributed lived experiences, drawing on data feminism and situated knowledge to design tools that treat marginalized users as decision makers rather than data points.
More broadly, I am interested in how power operates through data and interface design: whose experiences become data at all, who gets to define what a system shows, and who carries the consequences of those choices.
