I am a last year PhD student in the <a href='https://www.uu.nl/en/research/interaction/visualization-and-graphics'>Visualization and Graphics group</a> at <a href='https://www.uu.nl/en'>Utrecht University</a> under supervision of Dr. Evanthia Dimara and Prof. Alexandru Telea. 

I am Başak and prefer to be addressed with she/her pronouns.

I study how interactive visualization tools can support complex decision making -- decisions involving uncertainty, multiple criteria, constraints, and personal or social context. I combine empirical visualization research, interactive tool design, interviews, and quantitative and qualitative analysis, with projects on status-quo effects in visualization-aided choice, early-career decisions such as student loans and job seeking, and LGBTQ+ travel safety (QueerTravel, IEEE VIS 2026, conditionally accepted).
Working with real decision makers has increasingly drawn me to questions of power in data and design: whose experiences become data at all, who defines what a system shows, and who carries the consequences of those choices. My recent work draws on data feminism and situated knowledge to combine official indicators with community-contributed lived experiences, treating marginalized people as decision makers rather than data points.
